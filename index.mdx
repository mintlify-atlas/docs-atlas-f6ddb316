Pico FIDO is open-source firmware that transforms a Raspberry Pi Pico or ESP32 microcontroller into a hardware security key. It implements CTAP 2.1, WebAuthn, U2F, OATH TOTP/HOTP, and Yubikey OTP — all on affordable, widely available hardware that you own and control.

## What Pico FIDO does

Pico FIDO turns commodity microcontrollers into USB security keys that work with any FIDO2-compatible service — GitHub, Google, Microsoft, and hundreds of others. When you plug in your device, it appears as a standard HID authenticator with no drivers required.

## Get started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose your hardware">
    Pick a supported board — Raspberry Pi Pico (RP2040), Pico 2 (RP2350), or an ESP32-S3 board. See [hardware support](/hardware-support) for the full list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download or build firmware">
    Download a pre-built UF2 from the [releases page](https://github.com/polhenarejos/pico-fido/releases), or [build from source](/building/raspberry-pi-pico) for custom VID/PID or feature flags.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Flash your device">
    Hold **BOOTSEL**, plug in your Pico, and copy the `.uf2` file to the USB mass storage device. For ESP32-S3, use the [ESP32 Flasher](https://www.picokeys.com/esp32-flasher/).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Register as a passkey">
    Your device is immediately detected by all major browsers and operating systems as a standard USB HID security key — no drivers needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  For the best experience managing your device — including LED customization, credential management, and firmware updates — use the [PicoKey App](https://www.picokeys.com/picokeyapp/).
</Note>
