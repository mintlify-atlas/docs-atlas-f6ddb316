Pico FIDO is available under two editions. The Community Edition is free and open source under AGPLv3 — suitable for individuals, researchers, and internal evaluation. The Enterprise Edition is a proprietary license for organizations that need production-grade deployment, fleet management, corporate policy enforcement, or OEM integration without AGPLv3 disclosure obligations. This page explains what each edition covers and how to request a commercial license.

<Note>
  The Community Edition remains AGPLv3 and will continue to exist. Choosing the Enterprise Edition does not affect your ability to use, study, or modify the Community Edition under its existing open-source terms.
</Note>

## Editions at a glance

| | Community Edition | Enterprise Edition |
|---|---|---|
| **License** | AGPLv3 (open source) | Proprietary (commercial) |
| **Cost** | Free | Scoped and priced per customer |
| **Source disclosure** | Required for distributed or network-accessible modifications | Not required |
| **Support / SLA** | None | Optional (see below) |
| **Enterprise features** | Not included | Available as optional components |

### Community Edition

The Community Edition is intended for:

- Individual users and researchers
- Evaluation and prototyping
- Internal lab and security testing

You may read, modify, and run the source code. If you distribute modified firmware or binaries to third parties, or run a modified version as a network-accessible service, you must provide the corresponding source code to recipients — as required by AGPLv3.

### Enterprise Edition

The Enterprise Edition is for organizations that need to:

- Deploy at scale across multiple devices, users, or teams
- Integrate Pico FIDO into their own physical product or appliance
- Run it as an internal virtualized or private-cloud service for multiple teams or tenants
- Enforce internal security policy (admin vs. user roles, mandatory PIN rules, secure offboarding and revocation)
- Avoid AGPLv3 source disclosure obligations for their own modifications and integration code

## Base license package

Every Enterprise license always includes:

- **Commercial license (proprietary).** You may run and integrate the software in production — including virtualized and internal-cloud deployments — without being required to disclose derivative source code under AGPLv3.
- **Official signed builds.** You receive signed builds from the original developer so you can verify integrity and provenance.
- **Onboarding call (up to 1 hour).** A live remote session to get you from "we have it" to "it's running in our environment" with minimal guesswork.

## Licensing models

- **Internal Use License** — Internal production use within one legal entity, including internal private-cloud and virtualized deployments for multiple internal teams. Optional enterprise components can be added as needed.
- **OEM / Redistribution / Service License** — Integration into a product or appliance you ship to customers, or operating Pico FIDO as a managed service or hosted feature for external clients or third parties. Optional enterprise components (attestation branding, PQC, HD key derivation, multi-tenant hardening, audit trail, etc.) can be added as required.

Pricing depends on scope, fleet size, number of users or tenants, regulatory requirements, and which optional components you select.
